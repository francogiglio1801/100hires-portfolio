## Core Thesis

AI search engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT Search, Google AIO) depend on being able to crawl, understand, and index your content before they can cite it. Most GEO discussion skips this foundational layer entirely.

## Technical Foundations for LLM Visibility

**1. Crawlability for AI Bots**
- Verify that `robots.txt` doesn't block: `GPTBot`, `PerplexityBot`, `ClaudeBot`, `Anthropic-AI`, `Google-Extended`
- Many sites blocked AI crawlers to "protect content" from training data, inadvertently making themselves uncitable by AI search
- Aleyda's recommendation: allow AI search bots unless there's a specific legal reason not to

**2. Structured Data as AI Navigation Maps**
- Schema.org markup helps AI systems understand content hierarchy and relationships
- Most critical schemas for AI citation: `Article`, `FAQPage`, `HowTo`, `Person` (for author E-E-A-T), `Organization`
- AI systems use schema to understand who wrote what, what the content claims, and whether claims are attributed to verifiable entities

**3. Internal Linking as Knowledge Graph**
- LLMs build a context model of your site during crawling
- Dense, coherent internal linking helps establish topical clusters
- Orphaned pages (no internal links) are rarely cited — AI systems can't establish their relationship to site authority

**4. Page Speed and Accessibility**
- Slow pages are crawled less frequently by all bots, including AI search bots
- Core Web Vitals affect crawl budget: prioritize fastest-loading pages for key content
- Accessible HTML (proper heading hierarchy, semantic elements) is easier for AI parsers

## The eCommerce Angle

Aleyda addresses eCommerce SEO in the AI-heavy SERP environment:
- Product schema remains critical for Shopping visibility and AIO product carousels
- Review schema with verified sources is weighted heavily in product AIOs
- Category pages with genuine editorial content (not just product grids) appear in AIOs for "best X" queries
