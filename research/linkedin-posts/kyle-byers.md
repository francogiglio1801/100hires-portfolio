## The Semrush AI Content Workflow

**1. AI for Research and Outlines**
- Use AI to analyze top-ranking content and identify topic gaps
- Generate initial keyword clusters and content briefs
- Identify internal linking opportunities across existing content library

**2. AI for First Drafts**
- ContentShake AI (Semrush's own tool) generates drafts informed by real keyword data
- Drafts scored against Semrush's SEO Writing Assistant for readability and SEO completeness
- First drafts explicitly labeled as "starting points" — never published as-is

**3. Human Expert Review (Non-Negotiable)**
- Every article reviewed by a subject matter expert for accuracy
- Fact verification: all statistics sourced and verified
- Brand voice pass: AI drafts often lack the "Semrush" editorial personality
- E-E-A-T layer: author attribution, expert quotes, original examples

**4. Post-Publish Monitoring**
- Performance tracked against historical baselines
- AI-generated content A/B tested against human-written control articles
- Flagging system for articles with declining engagement metrics

## The Accuracy Problem at Scale

> "At the volume we publish, a 1% hallucination rate in AI-generated content is not acceptable. If we publish 500 articles a month and 1% contain a fabricated statistic, that's 5 articles per month with false information reaching millions of readers."

Specific failure modes Semrush has caught in AI drafts:
- **Fabricated statistics**: AI citing studies that don't exist or misquoting real studies
- **Outdated information**: AI training cutoffs mean recommendations based on old algorithm states
- **Tool feature errors**: AI describing Semrush's own features incorrectly
- **Competitive claim errors**: AI making factually wrong claims about competitor tools

---

## Scale Considerations

Key operational insights from managing AI content at Semrush scale:
- **Editorial calendar discipline**: AI increases output capacity, so editorial strategy becomes more important (not less) — you need all content serving the overall topical authority map
- **Duplicate content risk**: At scale, AI drafts from similar prompts can produce similar content — deduplication checks required before publishing
- **Internal link management**: More content = more linking opportunities, but also more risk of linking chaos

## Key Quotes

> "AI content tools don't make content strategy easier. They make execution faster and strategy more important. If you don't have a clear content strategy, AI just lets you create more of the wrong content faster."

> "The question isn't 'can AI write this article?' The question is 'will this article actually help our audience?' AI can answer the first question. Only a human who understands the audience can answer the second."

> "We build the tools that help people do this work. That means we have to do it ourselves, and we have to do it right. Our content is also our product demonstration."

## Key Takeaways

- Enterprise AI content requires strict human review: accuracy, voice, E-E-A-T layer
- At scale, 1% hallucination rate = unacceptable number of published errors
- Semrush workflow: ContentShake AI + SEO Writing Assistant + human expert review
- Editorial strategy becomes MORE important with AI, not less
- Duplicate content risk increases at scale — deduplication checks required
- AI increases content velocity but doesn't answer: does this actually help the audience?
