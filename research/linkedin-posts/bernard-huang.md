## What Is Topical Authority?

Bernard defines topical authority as Google's measure of how comprehensively a site covers a subject area — distinct from domain authority (link-based) or page authority (individual page signals).

> "Topical authority is Google's way of asking: 'Is this site the place I should send people who want to learn everything about [topic]?' It's a site-level signal, not a page-level signal."

Key distinction:
- **Domain Authority**: How many quality sites link to you (legacy metric)
- **Topical Authority**: How thoroughly your site covers every relevant subtopic in a niche
- These are independent — a new site with zero links but exhaustive topical coverage can outrank high-DA sites on topical queries

## The Information Gain Problem

Bernard introduces **information gain** as the critical concept for the AI content era:

> "The internet is now flooded with AI-generated content that says the same things in slightly different words. Google's systems have gotten very good at detecting 'consensus content' — content that just aggregates what everyone else has already said."

High information gain content:
- Original research or data
- First-hand experience or case studies
- Expert opinions that challenge conventional wisdom
- Synthesis of multiple sources into a new framework
- Specific examples, not general principles

Low information gain content (penalized in AI era):
- AI-generated summaries of existing articles
- Rephrased versions of competitor content
- Generic "what is X" pages with no novel angle

## Why AI Content Fails at Scale Without a Strategy

The fundamental paradox of AI content production:
- AI generates content at zero marginal cost
- Therefore, AI-generated "consensus" content is now abundant
- Abundant content has low information gain
- Low information gain = low topical authority signal
- Low topical authority = lower rankings, lower AIO inclusion probability

> "The companies using AI most effectively aren't using it to produce more of the same content faster. They're using it to do the research, synthesis, and structural work that frees up their human experts to add the information gain layer."

## Building Topical Authority: The Framework

**Step 1: Topic Map Construction**
- Identify the core topic
- Generate a comprehensive subtopic tree
- Ensure every major and secondary subtopic has dedicated coverage
- Gap analysis: which subtopics do top competitors cover that you don't?

**Step 2: Information Gain Audit**
- For each existing piece: what unique data, perspective, or synthesis does it contain?
- Low-information-gain articles: candidates for consolidation or enhancement, not deletion
- Enhancement strategy: add original data, expert quotes, case studies, or unique frameworks

**Step 3: Internal Linking Architecture**
- Link all subtopics back to the pillar page
- Link related subtopics to each other (creates a semantic cluster)
- Anchor text should reflect the subtopic keywords, not generic phrases

**Step 4: Freshness Signals**
- Regular updates to core pages signal topical accuracy maintenance
- New research or data > word count additions
- Date-stamp updates visibly (Google and users both value freshness signals)

## Topical Authority in the AI Overview Era

> "If you're included in an AI Overview, it's because Google's systems have identified you as an authority on that topic. The AIO is a downstream effect of topical authority, not a separate optimization target."

Clearscope's data on AIO-included pages:
- 87% had comprehensive topical coverage scores (measured by Clearscope's content grade)
- Average content length: 2,400 words (vs. 1,100 for non-included comparison pages)
- Structured markup present in 73% of included pages

## Key Quotes

> "Topical authority is the new domain authority. A site that comprehensively covers a niche will outrank a high-DA generalist site on every query in that niche, over time."

> "Information gain is the antidote to AI content commoditization. If your content adds nothing to the conversation that wasn't already out there, it will be treated as noise."

> "The brands winning in AI search are the brands known for something — a methodology, a dataset, a perspective. Not just known for 'covering marketing topics.'"

## Key Takeaways

- Topical authority is site-level and distinct from domain authority
- Information gain is the critical differentiator — content must add novel value
- AI-generated consensus content fails because it has zero information gain
- Framework: topic maps → information gain audit → internal linking → freshness signals
- AIO inclusion is downstream of topical authority, not a separate optimization target
- Clearscope data: 87% of AIO-included pages had high content completeness; avg 2,400 words
- Human experts add the information gain layer; AI handles structure and coverage analysis
