## Core Thesis

Many "Generative Engine Optimization" (GEO) tactics being promoted in 2025-2026 are actively harming the same sites' traditional Google SEO performance — and since ChatGPT and Perplexity still largely source from Google's index, you end up losing both.

> "Here's the uncomfortable truth: if your GEO strategy involves making your content shorter, more conversational, and AI-friendly at the expense of depth and expertise, you may be optimizing yourself out of both games."

---

## Section 1: What GEO Actually Is

Lily explains GEO in technical terms:
- **RAG Architecture**: Retrieval-Augmented Generation systems (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AIOs) retrieve candidate documents, then synthesize an answer
- Being "retrieved" depends on: ranking in the source index (usually Google) + semantic similarity to the query + content recency
- Being "used" in the synthesis depends on: clarity of content chunk, absence of conflicting signals, and visible E-E-A-T signals

> "When someone asks ChatGPT a question, it doesn't search the web the way you might imagine. It retrieves chunks of text — often from Bing/Google index if web search is on — and picks the chunks that most cleanly answer the question. Your job is to be the cleanest, most authoritative chunk."

---

## Section 2: The 5 GEO Tactics That Are Backfiring

**Tactic 1: Making content shorter and "AI-friendly"**
Sites cutting article length from 2,000+ words to 400-600 word summaries:
- Short-form articles see initial AIO inclusion spikes (easier to extract as a complete chunk)
- Within 60-90 days: organic rankings drop as topical depth score falls
- Net result: higher AIO visibility, 40-60% organic traffic loss
- Root cause: Google rewards content depth at the page level; AIOs pull from already-ranking pages

**Tactic 2: Removing expert opinions to sound "neutral"**
Some practitioners remove strong opinions or controversial takes to reduce bias risk:
- Sites doing this saw drops in E-E-A-T scores
- Unique perspectives and clear authorship differentiate content from LLM-generated consensus content flooding Google
- Root cause: Google is specifically trying to surface first-hand perspectives as a corrective to generic AI content

**Tactic 3: Optimizing for Perplexity/ChatGPT at the expense of Google structure**
Restructuring content away from traditional SEO toward conversational, direct-answer formats:
- Short-term: better AIO and Perplexity citation rates
- Medium-term (90+ days): loss of Google organic positions → loss of the very rankings that made you eligible for AI citation
- Root cause: "You can't hack your way into AIOs by making your content look like what AIOs produce. You get into AIOs by being authoritative enough that they cite you."

**Tactic 4: Removing structured data to "simplify" content**
Removing schema markup (FAQ, HowTo, Review) to create "cleaner" content for AI parsing:
- Sites with rich structured data are cited in AIOs at 2-3x the rate of equivalent unstructured content
- Root cause: Schema markup helps AI systems understand content structure — it's not a blocker, it's a map

**Tactic 5: Aggressive "answer box" optimization via thin content**
Creating dedicated 100-200 word "answer" pages targeting featured snippet/AIO slots:
- Featured snippet capture rates: initially high
- Duration: typically 30-60 days before Google demotes as thin content
- Root cause: Google's Helpful Content system evaluates page depth relative to the topic

## Section 3: What Actually Works

Lily's affirmative recommendations from sites that maintained or grew traffic through 2025-2026:

1. **Go deeper, not shallower**: The sites winning AIOs have the most comprehensive content — AIOs pull from the richest source available
2. **Build real author identity**: Author schema, author pages with credentials, consistent authorship — all correlated with AIO inclusion
3. **Original data and research**: Content citing original surveys or first-party data is cited in AIOs at dramatically higher rates
4. **Keep structured markup**: FAQ, HowTo, Speakable schemas remain strong signals
5. **Answer the follow-up question**: Structure content to answer primary query AND 2-3 most common follow-up questions

## Section 4: Why SEO = AI Search Foundation

> "ChatGPT (with web search), Perplexity, and Google AIOs collectively draw from a pool that is 85-90% correlated with Google's top-10 organic results. Winning traditional SEO is a prerequisite for AI search visibility, not an alternative to it."

## Key Quotes

> "The algorithm is getting better at detecting content that was written to rank rather than written to help. The irony is that 'written to rank' now includes written to rank in AIOs."

> "Your GEO strategy is your SEO strategy. If those are two different documents in your company, you have a problem."

> "The sites I've seen hold up best through 2025 and into 2026 are the ones that doubled down on original research, real expert perspectives, and genuine topical coverage."

---

## Key Takeaways

- GEO and SEO are not separate strategies — GEO without SEO is unsustainable
- 5 GEO tactics actively harming sites: shortening content, removing expert voice, restructuring away from SEO, removing schema, thin answer-box pages
- RAG systems source from Google-indexed content → Google ranking is the prerequisite for AI citation
- What works: depth, real author identity, original data, structured markup, answer the follow-up question
- GEO-only tactics often show short-term gains (30-90 days) before backfiring
