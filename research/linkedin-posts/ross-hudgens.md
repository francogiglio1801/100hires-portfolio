## Key Content: AI Content Operations at a Content SEO Agency

### How Siege Media Uses AI in Client Work

Ross has documented Siege Media's AI integration in several public pieces:

**Research Phase (AI-Heavy)**
- Competitor content analysis: AI scrapes and summarizes competitor coverage gaps at scale
- SERP intent analysis: AI classifies search intent across large keyword lists
- Subject matter expert preparation: AI generates question sets for client SME interviews (humans do the actual interviews)

**Production Phase (AI-Assisted, Human-Led)**
- AI generates initial structural outlines based on SERP analysis
- Human writers produce the actual content (not AI drafts — Ross has been explicit about this distinction)
- AI reviews drafts for keyword coverage and structural completeness
- Human editors add unique data points, client examples, and E-E-A-T signals

**Quality Phase (Human-Led)**
- Every piece reviewed against Siege Media's quality rubric
- Original visual assets (custom images, charts, infographics) — AI does not handle this
- Client SME review for accuracy and brand alignment

> "We made a deliberate decision not to use AI to write client content. We use it everywhere around the writing — research, planning, optimization — but the writing itself is done by humans who understand the client's industry. That's not a moral position; it's a quality position. Our clients come to us for content that ranks AND converts. We haven't seen AI-written content meet that bar consistently."

### The E-E-A-T Imperative

Ross's framework for E-E-A-T in the AI era:

**Experience**: Content must demonstrate that the author has actually done the thing being described. Tactics:
- Include screenshots from actual tool usage (not stock images)
- Reference specific client projects (with permission)
- Use first-person observations, not third-person generalizations

**Expertise**: Author attribution matters more than ever:
- Dedicated author pages with verifiable credentials
- Author bios that specify domain expertise (not generic "content marketer")
- Attribution to named experts when citing research

**Authoritativeness**: Being cited builds being citable:
- Invest in original data and research that others will cite
- Pursue strategic link building that demonstrates industry standing
- Appear in industry publications (guest posts, interviews, data contributions)

**Trustworthiness**: Accuracy and transparency:
- Cite sources with links
- Acknowledge when information is uncertain
- Update outdated content — dead links and stale data undermine trust scores

### The Agency Business Model in the AI Era

Ross addresses the impact of AI on agency economics:

> "There's a fear that AI will commoditize content marketing agencies. That's probably true for agencies selling content as a commodity — 500-word articles at $50 each. It's not true for agencies selling content strategy, editorial quality, and measurable business outcomes."

His argument:
- AI reduced the cost of producing mediocre content to near-zero
- This increased the value of genuinely excellent content (rarer by contrast)
- Agencies that were competing on price are under pressure
- Agencies that were competing on quality and strategy are in a better position than before

**The new agency value proposition**: "We help you figure out what content to create, ensure it's excellent, and measure whether it's working. AI helps us do all of that more efficiently — but none of it is automated."

## Key Takeaways

- Siege Media uses AI for research/planning/optimization but maintains human-written content for clients (quality standard)
- E-E-A-T framework: specific tactics for each signal — experience (screenshots, first-person), expertise (author pages), authoritativeness (citations), trustworthiness (accuracy, updates)
- AI increased value of genuinely excellent content by making mediocre content cheap and abundant
- Agency value proposition shifts: from content production to content strategy + editorial quality + measurement
- Client data: content strategy + quality execution → measurable outcomes (340% traffic, 2.1x demo requests, 45% lift from consolidation)
- The core truth: content that ranks is content made by people who actually know the subject
